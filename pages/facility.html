CRUISE SHIP GALLEY

The centerpiece of the entire facility is the well-equipped glass-walled kitchen which replicates, as closely as practicable, the equipment used on cruise ships. As a training and assessment galley, various international cruise and land base companies had used this kitchen to test the cooking skills of chef applicants and to train newly hired chefs before joining the ships. Its glass walls had also witnessed students and industry practitioners who successfully passed competency assessments in catering, cookery and bread and pastry.

MERCHANT SHIP GALLEY

This galley is specifically designed to meet the requirements for training catering crew for merchant ships. Each compartment resembles that of a small merchant ship galley and is fully equipped to be able to conduct trainings for Cooks and Chief Cooks of merchant ships. This galley is also used as a competency assessment venue Ships Cooks. It likewise doubles as a production galley for the restaurant operation of The Commodoreâ€™s Table.

THE COMMODORE'S TABLE

Manned by experienced F&B staff, the full workflow is encompassed by allowing them to manage the room and table setup, order-taking, food and beverage service, bussing, as well as cleaning and equipment maintenance. As part of our training courses, trainees are assigned to work alongside the regular staff so that they may put theory into practice. Visitors are welcome on business days, where they may relax and view both the busy kitchen and the skyline views and beyond, through floor to ceiling glass walls, while partaking of the food and beverage service at moderate prices.

THE COMMODORE'S BAR

The bar is located in the dining room and is equipped with all the tools and equipment necessary to train bartenders; covering storage, preparation and service of wines, spirits, alcoholic and non-alcoholic beverages. Coffee and tea-making facilities provide a range of hot beverages including brewed, espresso and cappuccino coffees, and a selection of herbal and regular teas.

THE CRUISE CABIN

Adjacent to the classroom and viewable through a sliding glass wall is a mock cruise ship cabin. Complete with an integral bathroom, this allows trainees to put into practice the proper room cleaning and setup procedures. This all leads to a better understanding of an efficient housekeeping workflow sequence.

THE PORTSIDE LECTURE ROOM

A 401 square feet room that features a view of the city and free wi-fi connection. Seating arrangements may vary: for a classroom set up this room can accommodate up to 30 students, Portside can also hold a maximum of 20 people for a dining set up and 12-14 pax for conferences and other meetings.

THE STARBOARD LECTURE ROOM

Starboard offers a wide space of 377 square feet. For a classroom set up, this room can accommodate a maximum of 24 participants, 20 people for dining and 12-14 pax for conferences and meetings. This room also has movable walls and thus can be opened to accommodate bigger groups.

THE BRIDGE DINING ROOM

When the walls of Starboard are opened, there can be space for 80 - 90 trainees' theater style; for dining set up 50-60 diners.
